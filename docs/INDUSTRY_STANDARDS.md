# Construction Industry Standards Reference

Quick reference for industry standards implemented in ConstructAI MEP analysis.

---

## CSI MasterFormat 2016

**Organization**: Construction Specifications Institute (CSI)  
**Purpose**: Standard taxonomy for organizing construction specifications

### Key Divisions (MEP Focus):
- **Division 21**: Fire Suppression
- **Division 22**: Plumbing
- **Division 23**: Heating, Ventilating, and Air Conditioning (HVAC)
- **Division 25**: Integrated Automation
- **Division 26**: Electrical
- **Division 27**: Communications
- **Division 28**: Electronic Safety and Security

**Reference**: https://www.csiresources.org/standards/masterformat

## HVAC Standards

### ASHRAE (American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers)

**Key Standards**:
- **ASHRAE 90.1**: Energy Standard for Buildings (efficiency requirements)
- **ASHRAE 62.1**: Ventilation for Acceptable Indoor Air Quality
- **ASHRAE 55**: Thermal Environmental Conditions for Human Occupancy

**Equipment Nomenclature**:
- AHU: Air Handling Unit
- RTU: Rooftop Unit
- FCU: Fan Coil Unit
- VAV: Variable Air Volume
- MAU: Make-Up Air Unit
- VRF: Variable Refrigerant Flow
- VRV: Variable Refrigerant Volume

**Capacity Units**:
- Ton: Cooling capacity (1 ton = 12,000 BTU/hr)
- BTU/hr: British Thermal Units per hour
- MBH: Thousand BTU per hour
- CFM: Cubic Feet per Minute (airflow)
- GPM: Gallons Per Minute (water flow)

**Efficiency Ratings**:
- **SEER**: Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio (cooling efficiency)
- **EER**: Energy Efficiency Ratio (cooling efficiency at specific conditions)
- **COP**: Coefficient of Performance (heat pump efficiency)
- **HSPF**: Heating Seasonal Performance Factor (heat pump heating)
- **AFUE**: Annual Fuel Utilization Efficiency (furnace/boiler)

**Reference**: https://www.ashrae.org

---

### SMACNA (Sheet Metal and Air Conditioning Contractors' National Association)

**Purpose**: Ductwork fabrication and installation standards

**Key Standards**:
- HVAC Duct Construction Standards
- Duct System Leakage Testing
- Rectangular Industrial Duct Construction Standards

**Duct Types**:
- Rectangular: Commercial buildings
- Round: Industrial, residential
- Oval: Space-constrained areas
- Spiral: Long runs, aesthetics

**Materials**:
- Galvanized steel (most common)
- Stainless steel (corrosive environments)
- Aluminum (coastal areas)
- Flex duct (final connections)

**Reference**: https://www.smacna.org

---

### IMC (International Mechanical Code)

**Organization**: International Code Council (ICC)  
**Purpose**: Model mechanical code for building construction

**Scope**:
- HVAC system installation
- Ventilation requirements
- Combustion air
- Chimneys and vents
- Equipment standards

**Reference**: https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IMC2021P1

---

### AHRI (Air-Conditioning, Heating, and Refrigeration Institute)

**Purpose**: Equipment certification and rating standards

**Key Standards**:
- AHRI 210/240: Unitary Air Conditioners and Heat Pumps
- AHRI 340/360: Commercial and Industrial Unitary Equipment
- AHRI 550/590: Chillers

**Certification Programs**:
- Equipment performance verification
- Energy efficiency ratings (SEER, EER, COP)
- Sound ratings

**Reference**: https://www.ahrinet.org

## Plumbing Standards

### IPC (International Plumbing Code)

**Organization**: International Code Council (ICC)  
**Purpose**: Model plumbing code for building construction

**Key Chapters**:
- **Chapter 4**: Fixtures, Faucets and Fixture Fittings
- **Chapter 6**: Water Supply and Distribution
- **Chapter 7**: Sanitary Drainage
- **Chapter 9**: Vents

**Fixture Abbreviations**:
- **WC**: Water Closet (toilet)
- **LAV**: Lavatory (sink)
- **UR**: Urinal
- **DF**: Drinking Fountain
- **FD**: Floor Drain
- **CO**: Cleanout
- **HB**: Hose Bibb

**Fixture Units**:
- Measurement of drainage load
- WC: 4 fixture units
- LAV: 1 fixture unit
- Shower: 2 fixture units

**Reference**: https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IPC2021P1

---

### UPC (Uniform Plumbing Code)

**Organization**: International Association of Plumbing and Mechanical Officials (IAPMO)  
**Purpose**: Model plumbing code (used primarily in western US)

**Similar Scope to IPC**:
- Fixture requirements
- Water supply systems
- Drainage systems
- Venting

**Key Difference**: Venting methods vary from IPC

**Reference**: https://www.iapmo.org

### Pipe Material Standards

#### ASTM (American Society for Testing and Materials)

**Copper Pipe**:
- **ASTM B88**: Seamless Copper Water Tube
  - Type K: Heavy wall (underground)
  - Type L: Medium wall (interior water supply)
  - Type M: Thin wall (interior water supply)

**Plastic Pipe**:
- **ASTM D2665**: PVC Pipe (Drain, Waste, Vent)
- **ASTM D2846**: CPVC Pipe (Hot and Cold Water Distribution)
- **ASTM F876**: PEX Tubing (Cross-linked Polyethylene)

**Cast Iron**:
- **ASTM A74**: Cast Iron Soil Pipe and Fittings

**Steel**:
- **ASTM A53**: Galvanized Steel Pipe

## Water Supply Specifications

### Pressure Standards:

**Minimum Pressure** (IPC 604.3):
- Residential: 20 PSI minimum at highest fixture
- Commercial: 15 PSI minimum at outlets

**Maximum Pressure** (IPC 604.8):
- 80 PSI maximum static pressure
- Pressure-reducing valve required above 80 PSI

### Flow Rate Standards:

**Fixture Flow Rates** (EPA WaterSense):
- Showerheads: 2.0 GPM maximum (federal standard)
- Kitchen faucets: 2.2 GPM maximum
- Bathroom faucets: 1.5 GPM maximum
- Water closets: 1.6 GPF (gallons per flush) maximum
- Urinals: 1.0 GPF maximum

---

## Drainage Specifications

### Pipe Sizing (IPC Chapter 7):

**Drainage Fixture Units (DFU)**:
- Determines required pipe diameter
- Based on fixture type and load

**Minimum Drain Sizes**:
- Water closet: 3" minimum
- Shower: 2" minimum
- Lavatory: 1-1/4" minimum (typical 1-1/2")
- Kitchen sink: 1-1/2" minimum

**Slope Requirements**:
- Pipes ≤3" diameter: 1/4" per foot minimum
- Pipes ≥4" diameter: 1/8" per foot minimum

## Typical Equipment Specifications

### HVAC Equipment Sizes:

**Residential**:
- Air conditioners: 1.5 - 5 tons
- Furnaces: 40,000 - 120,000 BTU/hr
- Heat pumps: 2 - 5 tons

**Commercial**:
- Rooftop units: 3 - 50 tons
- Chillers: 50 - 1000+ tons
- Air handlers: 1,000 - 50,000 CFM
- Boilers: 100,000 - 10,000,000 BTU/hr

### Plumbing Equipment:

**Water Heaters**:
- Residential tank: 30 - 80 gallons
- Commercial tank: 80 - 500+ gallons
- Tankless: 150,000 - 200,000 BTU/hr (residential)

**Pumps**:
- Domestic water: 5 - 100 GPM
- Booster pumps: 15 - 75 PSI boost
